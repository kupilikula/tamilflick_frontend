Tribute to J. P. Chandrababu

Joseph Panimayadas Chandrababu Rodriguez (1927-1974) was a Tamil artist who sang and danced to a number of Tamil songs that have become classics. Chandrababu had an intellectual and philosophical spirit that shines through in these songs, even though they were written by other people — often Kannadasan.

Chandrababu was a multi-faceted artist. He could sing, dance, act and do slap-stick comedy. He lived an eventful life which included a suicide attempt, financial ruin, marriage and divorce. He starred in movies with M.G. Ramachandran and Sivaji Ganesan.

Let’s start with one of my favorite Chandrababu songs — ‘பிறக்கும் போதும் அழுகின்றாய்’ from the movie ‘கவலையில்லா மனிதன்’ (1960). It’s a relatively simple but meaningful song. The video starts with shots of a turbulent ocean and of dark clouds. Chandrababu stands in what looks like a graveyard with a garden and sings the opening lines like a philosopher delivering a sermon. There are shots of a mother with a newborn baby. He then strolls around the garden for the rest of the video, pausing and posing, moving slowly and expressing sorrow and bewilderment.

Chandrababu is an expert at expressing emotions with his body, in the way he walks, the way he poses, the movement of his arms and the expressions on his face. This is also true of his dancing, as exemplified by this next song and video — ‘குங்கும பூவே, கொஞ்சும் புறாவே’ from the movie ‘மரகதம்’ (1959). This is a light-hearted and playful song with Chandrababu and Jamuna Rani singing while the video has Chandrababu and Sandhya prancing and dancing around a garden. The lyrics were written by Ku. Ma. Balasubramaniam.
